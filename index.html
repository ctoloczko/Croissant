Croissant

A croissant is a buttery, flaky, and crescent-shaped pastry originating from France, known for its layered texture and rich flavor. It is made from a laminated dough, which involves folding butter into the dough multiple times to create thin layers of dough separated by butter. When baked, the water in the butter evaporates, causing the dough to puff up and create the croissant's characteristic flaky layers.

Fun fact

Despite their strong association with France, croissants are believed to have been inspired by the Austrian kipferl, a crescent-shaped pastry. They became popular in France after being introduced by Austrian bakers in the 19th century.
